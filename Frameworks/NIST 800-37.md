# NIST 800-37 Risk Mgmt Framework

_**🚨 The Core Idea**_

The government needs a standard way to protect its computer systems from hackers and accidents. NIST 800-37 is the rulebook that says how to think about and manage that risk. Not just once, but continuously.

### The 7 Steps (Securing your house)
- 📝 Prepare
Before you do anything, get organized. Who owns the house? What are we protecting? What's our budget for locks?
- 🗂️ Categorize
How valuable is what's inside? Is it a garden shed or a bank vault? Low / Medium / High sensitivity.
- 🔒 Select Controls
Pick the right locks, alarms, and cameras based on how valuable your stuff is. (These come from a menu called NIST 800-53.)
- 🔧 Implement
Install the locks, alarms and cameras needed
-  ✅ Assess
Hire someone to test the locks. Do they actually work? Are there gaps?
- 🪪 Authorize
A senior official (the "Authorizing Official") reviews everything and signs off: "Yes, I accept the remaining risk. Go live."
-  🔁 Monitor
Keep watching. Locks wear out, new threats emerge. This is ongoing, not a one-time checkbox.

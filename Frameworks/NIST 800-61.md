What Is It?
If NIST 800-37 is about preventing fires, then NIST 800-61 is the fire department's playbook — what to do when something goes wrong.
It's the Computer Security Incident Handling Guide. It gives organizations a standard way to detect, respond to, and recover from security incidents.
----

The 4 Phases (The Fire Department Analogy)
1. 🏋️ Preparation

Before any fire happens — train the crew, buy the trucks, install the alarms, write the playbooks.

Build your SIEM, write runbooks, define what "incident" even means, establish your on-call rotations.

2. 🚨 Detection & Analysis

The alarm goes off. Is it a real fire or burnt toast?

Triage alerts, correlate logs, determine severity, confirm it's a real incident — not a false positive.

3. 🧯 Containment, Eradication & Recovery

Contain — Shut the door so fire doesn't spread (isolate the infected host)
Eradicate — Put the fire out (remove malware, patch the vuln)
Recover — Rebuild what burned (restore systems, verify clean)

4. 📋 Post-Incident Activity

After the fire — what happened, why, and how do we stop it next time?

Write the incident report, do the lessons-learned meeting, update playbooks.

Why This Is Critical for SOC Analysts
This isn't just a government document — it's essentially the job description of a SOC analyst written out as a framework.

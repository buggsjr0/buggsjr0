# NIST 800-61 <br/>

It's the Computer Security Incident Handling Guide. It gives organizations a standard way to detect, respond to, and recover from security incidents.<br>
If NIST 800-37 is about preventing fires, then NIST 800-61 is the fire department's playbook: what to do when something goes wrong.<br>

---

The 4 Phases (The Fire Department Analogy)
1. 🏋️ Preparation<br>
Before any fire happens — train the crew, buy the trucks, install the alarms, write the playbooks.<br>
Build your SIEM, write runbooks, define what "incident" even means, establish your on-call rotations.<br>

2. 🚨 Detection & Analysis<br>
The alarm goes off. Is it a real fire or burnt toast?<br>
Triage alerts, correlate logs, determine severity, confirm it's a real incident and not a false positive.<br>

3. 🧯 Containment, Eradication & Recovery<br>
Contain — Shut the door so fire doesn't spread (isolate the infected host)<br>
Eradicate — Put the fire out (remove malware, patch the vuln)<br>
Recover — Rebuild what burned (restore systems, verify clean)<br>

4. 📋 Post-Incident Activity
After the fire — what happened, why, and how do we stop it next time?<br>
Write the incident report, do the lessons-learned meeting, update playbooks.<br>
---

Why This Is Critical for SOC Analysts<br>
This isn't just a government document, it's essentially the job description of a SOC analyst written out as a framework.

### 800-61 In Use
| SOC Activity | Where 800-61 Lives
| ----- | ----- |
| Monitoring SIEM alerts| Detection & Analysis |
| Triaging tickets by severity | Detection & Analysis |
| Isolating a compromised endpoint	 | Containment |
| Running malware scans & remediation | Eradication |
| Writing incident tickets & reports | Post-Incident |
| Building/updating runbooks	 | Preparation |
| Threat hunting | Detection & Analysis |

---
| Severity Tiers | (How SOCs Prioritize)
| ----- | ----- |
| 🔴  CRITICAL | Active breach, data exfiltration in progress → drop everything |
| 🟠  HIGH     | Ransomware, privileged account compromise → respond now |
| 🟡  MEDIUM   | Malware detected, contained → respond within hours |
| 🟢  LOW      | Policy violation, recon activity → handle in queue |

---

### Key Takeaway 
NIST 800-61 is the theoretical backbone behind everything a SOC analyst woud do on a shift. Open a ticket, isolate the host, escalate to a Tier 2 or write an after action report. All of these actions are executing this framework.
